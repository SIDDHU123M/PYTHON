Here's the order of precedence for operators in programming, listed from high to low:

1. Parentheses `()`

2. Exponentiation `**`

3. Unary plus `+` and unary minus `-` (as in `-x`)

4. Multiplication `*`, division `/`, and modulo `%`

5. Addition `+` and subtraction `-`

6. Bitwise shift left `<<` and bitwise shift right `>>`

7. Bitwise AND `&`

8. Bitwise XOR `^`

9. Bitwise OR `|`

10. Comparison operators (`<`, `<=`, `>`, `>=`, `==`, `!=`)

11. Logical NOT `not`

12. Logical AND `and`

13. Logical OR `or`
